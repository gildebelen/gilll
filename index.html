Engine Parts

The engine is the heart of a vehicle. Here are some of the essential engine parts and how to use or maintain them.

Spark Plug

The spark plug ignites the fuel-air mixture in the engine cylinders. Check and replace it regularly for efficient combustion.

Air Filter

An air filter keeps debris out of the engine. Replacing it as per maintenance schedule improves engine performance.

Fuel Injector

Fuel injectors deliver fuel to the engine in a fine mist. Cleaning them ensures optimal fuel efficiency.

Suspension System

The suspension system provides a smooth and controlled ride. Learn about the main suspension components.

Shock Absorber

Shock absorbers control the impact of bumps, providing a stable ride. Inspect for leaks and replace if worn out.

Coil Spring

Coil springs support the vehicle's weight and absorb road shock. Look for sagging or cracks during inspection.

Control Arm

The control arm connects the wheel to the frame, allowing smooth wheel movement. Check bushings for wear.

Electrical System

The electrical system powers essential components. Here’s an overview of key parts and their maintenance.

Battery

The battery provides power to start the engine. Check voltage and clean terminals to ensure proper function.

Alternator

The alternator charges the battery and powers the electrical system. Test for consistent output voltage.

Fuse Box

The fuse box protects electrical circuits. Replace any blown fuses to maintain component function.

Braking System

A functional braking system is critical for safety. Here’s how to maintain key brake components.

Brake Pads

Brake pads create friction to stop the car. Replace when worn to maintain braking efficiency.

Brake Rotor

Brake rotors work with pads to slow the vehicle. Resurface or replace if they show uneven wear.

Brake Fluid

Brake fluid transmits force in the braking system. Change it as per schedule to avoid contamination.
